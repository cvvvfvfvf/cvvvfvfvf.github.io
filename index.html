About

Hi, I am Jingyi Li, an undergraduate student majoring in Data Science at the School of Mathematics, Tianjin University.

I am passionate about discovering underlying patterns in complex data. My research focuses on leveraging mathematical modeling and machine learning to solve real-world problems.

Research Interests

  • High-Dimensional Statistical Modeling: Mediation analysis, causal inference, and survival data frameworks.
  • Environmental Data Science: Predictive modeling of oceanographic variables using machine learning and time series analysis.
  • Mathematical Combinatorics: Integer partitions, generating functions, and number theory applications.

Research Experience

Predictability Analysis of Marine Environmental Variables

Time: Continuous Observation Period
Keywords: Time Series Modeling, XGBoost, Scikit-learn, Regression Analysis
Assessed the predictability of salinity and five types of nutrients (phosphate, ammonia nitrogen, nitrite, nitrate, inorganic nitrogen) in coastal waters. Developed a machine learning pipeline utilizing regression analysis and cluster analysis based on observation data from coastal marine environmental monitoring stations (e.g., FB_TJST01-03) to improve environmental forecasting models.

Mediation Analysis for High-Dimensional Survival Data

Application: DNA Information Storage Reliability
Keywords: SCAD Penalty, BIC Tuning, Causal Inference, Joint Significance Testing
Constructed a high-dimensional survival data mediation analysis framework, treating DNA information failure time as the survival outcome. Implemented parameter tuning via Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC) over the SCAD penalty space, and conducted rigorous joint significance testing to identify key mechanistic pathways in DNA storage degradation.
